# reef-propagation-log

Open logbook of a home marine aquarium built for propagation experiments
with soft corals and zoanthids. Records setup, water chemistry, protocols,
results, and failures.

**Started July 2026. No results yet.**

---

## Who

A high-school student in Jiangsu, China, who has kept aquariums for
several years. US applicant, planning to study environmental /
ecological engineering.

## Why

Around 90–95% of marine ornamental *species* in the aquarium trade are
still taken from the wild, because captive breeding is difficult for
most of them. My hobby is part of that demand. This log is an attempt
to work on the other side of it: making propagation cheaper and more
reproducible, and publishing everything openly so other hobbyists can
copy it.

*(Note: I am still verifying the exact trade statistics against primary
sources. Numbers here may be corrected later.)*

## Current status — 2026-07-19

Nothing is running yet at project scale. Where things actually stand:

- **Existing tank:** 50x30x30 side-filter, ~37.5 L. Heavy nuisance algae.
- **Suspected causes**, in order: old coral-bone filter media and old
  sand accumulating organics; too little live rock (~2.5 kg); light
  output far exceeding what the livestock needs.
- **Livestock:** 2 clownfish, 1 sea cucumber, 1 snail, zoanthids.
- **Planned upgrade:** a used ~45 cm cube, bought second-hand.

## Running experiments

**E01 — Light reduction vs. nuisance algae** (started 2026-07-19)
Reduced light to 40% intensity and 6 h photoperiod, all other
conditions unchanged. Photographing the same section of glass and rock
weekly. Single-variable, before/after, uncontrolled — this is a
diagnostic, not a controlled experiment, and it cannot separate light
from the slow decline of accumulated nutrients.

## Not yet decided

The main research question. Candidates under consideration:
fragment size and attachment method in soft corals; light dose vs.
growth; cryptofauna community assembly; copepod culture yield.
Hardware is compatible with all of them, so the tank is being built first.
